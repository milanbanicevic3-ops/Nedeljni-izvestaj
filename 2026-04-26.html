The Week in Three Points
Equities vs FX Divergence

S-P 500 hit all-time high of 7,165 on Friday. Nasdaq +2% on Intel earnings (+23.6%). Yet EUR/USD fell 1.5% this week, Gold corrected 2.5%, Silver lost 5%. Markets are pricing two different stories simultaneously: AI-earnings optimism in equities, geopolitical risk-premium and higher-for-longer in FX and metals. This divergence resolves through the central bank cascade April 28-30.

JPY Positioning at Historic Extreme

CFTC data released Friday confirms JPY net short at -94,460 contracts - the deepest in 19 years. Shorts increased by 10,524 in a single week. Ueda's G20 dovish remarks reduced April hike odds to 9% but the structural squeeze risk is the highest it has been since before the 2007 BoJ normalization cycle. The BoJ April 28 meeting is asymmetric: no hike changes nothing, any normalization signal triggers violent covering across all JPY crosses.

Consumer at Record Low Confidence

University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment fell to 49.8 Friday - the lowest reading since 1978. One-year inflation expectations jumped to 4.7%. Five-year at 3.5%. US gasoline prices 40% above pre-war levels. This is the tension underneath the equity ATH: Main Street is not participating in the market optimism. Consumer Confidence index on Monday and US GDP + PCE on Thursday will be the fundamental test.
